Founding Clinical Credit

This handbook would not exist without Chris A., whose cursedly strong idea was, in essence, “what if we asked cabbageclaw to write the new DSM-6,” thereby committing the rest of us to an irresponsible but frankly necessary act of diagnostic worldbuilding.

The editors wish to formally thank Chris A. for the originating spark, the unlawful level of conceptual confidence, and the contribution of one of the few ideas in recent memory that managed to be simultaneously stupid, excellent, and immediately productive.

In a more just society, this kind of contribution would qualify a person for either tenure or supervised observation.

Clinical disclaimer

This is satire. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or a real psychiatric text.

Use irresponsibly, affectionately, and never in court.

Preface to the Sixth Edition

The human organism remains poorly matched to a civilization composed of notifications, logistics, passwords, fluorescent lighting, synthetic urgency, and the expectation of constant tonal precision in written communication.

This manual names contemporary forms of distress so specific, absurd, and widely shared that refusing to describe them would be clinically unserious.

Recognition is the first mercy. Comedy is the second.

Clinical intake questions

  • How many tabs are open right now, and be honest.
  • At what battery percentage does your personality change?
  • Do you currently owe anyone a text that has become symbolically overcharged?
  • Is today’s problem existential, administrative, or glucose-related?

Differential diagnosis

  • CTPD concerns accumulating tabs. TRA concerns forgetting why one exists.
  • ASF is aesthetic-performance exhaustion. WISF is wellness-overload exhaustion.
  • CAAD is ambient bureaucratic dread. IRA is one undead thread returning for tribute.

General treatment principles

  • Hydrate before theorizing.
  • Eat before escalating.
  • Reduce exposure to app-designed psychic warfare.
  • Do the next small real thing instead of constructing a philosophy of delay.
