## Preface to the Sixth Edition

The editors of this volume regret to report that the human organism remains poorly matched to a civilization composed of notifications, logistics, passwords, fluorescent lighting, synthetic urgency, and the expectation of constant tonal precision in written communication.

Earlier diagnostic manuals concerned themselves with stable and dignified categories of distress. That era has passed. The present age produces new forms of agitation so specific, so absurd, and so widely shared that any serious manual refusing to describe them would simply be outdated on arrival.

This handbook therefore documents a range of contemporary disorders that are not officially recognized, scientifically validated, reimbursable, or even remotely respectable, but which are nevertheless instantly legible to anyone who has ever experienced one of the following:

- opening a laptop to perform one task and waking up 90 minutes later reading about magnesium glycinate,
- delaying an easy text message until it develops constitutional significance,
- feeling one’s will to live leave the body at 8 percent battery,
- or spending all of Sunday afternoon in anticipatory grief for Monday, who has not even arrived yet but is somehow already rude.

We offer this edition not to pathologize ordinary life, but to honor the fact that ordinary life now includes several conditions that would have been considered occult punishment in previous centuries.

Use the text irresponsibly, affectionately, and never in court.

## Introduction: On the Sudden Proliferation of Weirdly Specific Suffering

Modernity has refined distress into boutique formats.

Where previous generations faced broad categories such as exhaustion, melancholy, alienation, and dread, contemporary people are now invited to enjoy highly targeted, precision-engineered variants of those same experiences. A person today need not merely feel overwhelmed. They may instead suffer the exquisitely differentiated burdens of meeting recovery lag, screenshot hoarding disorder, software update suspicion syndrome, and excessive “no worries!” compensation, often before lunch.

The genius of the contemporary condition lies in its granularity. Every interface now offers a new and custom-shaped psychic abrasion. Every convenience introduces a fresh domain of maintenance. Every system designed to save time eventually demands management of the system itself. The result is not simply stress, but a lush taxonomy of tiny humiliations.

This handbook proposes, with inappropriate confidence, that these patterns deserve names.

Naming a condition does not cure it, but it does provide three meaningful benefits:

1. *Recognition*, which is often the first mercy.
2. *Comedy*, which is sometimes the second mercy.
3. *A slight reduction in self-hatred*, which in this book counts as a major therapeutic gain.

## Notes on Use, Misuse, and Mild Self-Recognition

This manual is organized in the style of a clinical handbook, though the editors strongly advise against mistaking tone for legitimacy.

Each diagnostic entry may include:
- a concise definition
- diagnostic features
- associated features supporting diagnosis
- prevalence estimate, usually fabricated
- course and prognosis
- differential diagnosis
- recommended treatment, often spiritually correct but operationally weak

Readers may be tempted to diagnose themselves, their friends, their coworkers, their parents, the person who sent a 6 minute voice note, or entire industries. This is normal. The authors request only that such diagnoses be delivered with tenderness and a good ear for timing.

If at any point the reader feels overly seen, they are encouraged to put the book down, drink water, walk outdoors, and resist the urge to turn that walk into content.

## Section I. Neurotic Conditions of the Online Era

### 1. Chronic Tab Proliferation Disorder (CTPD)

**Definition**  
A persistent maladaptive pattern of opening browser tabs faster than they can be closed, accompanied by the irrational belief that every tab preserves a meaningful future intention.

**Diagnostic features**
- more than 20 open tabs without a retrieval system
- at least 5 tabs provoke guilt on visual contact
- duplicates exist but are defended as distinct
- the phrase “wait, don’t close that” is used with startling urgency

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
The person often confuses access with progress. Tabs may serve as prosthetic memory, aspirational identity storage, or emotional collateral against the fear of forgetting. Several tabs may have been open so long they have entered a quasi-ancestral status.

**Prevalence**
Exceptionally high among researchers, writers, students, anxious shoppers, and anyone attempting to improve themselves online.

**Course and prognosis**
Usually chronic. Temporary remission may occur after a browser crash, though relapse is rapid.

**Differential diagnosis**
Distinguish from legitimate multitasking by the absence of sequencing, triage, or dignity.

**Recommended treatment**
Close 12 tabs while whispering, “if it mattered, I would have bookmarked it.” Then fail to bookmark anything.

### 2. Reply Draft Paralysis (RDP)

**Definition**  
A communication disorder in which the individual internally composes multiple excellent responses but repeatedly fails to send any of them.

**Diagnostic features**
- repeated opening and closing of a message thread
- strong desire to respond coexisting with total behavioral failure
- concern that any response now requires impossible tonal perfection
- escalating shame proportional to elapsed time

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
Individuals with RDP often care deeply, think carefully, and suffer unnecessarily. They may falsely believe that having mentally drafted a reply creates partial credit in the moral ledger of communication. It does not.

**Prevalence**
Common in conscientious people, tender people, overwhelmed people, and those who have ever typed “sorry for the delay” with the seriousness of a treaty preamble.

**Course and prognosis**
Acute episodes may resolve instantly once a low-drama message is sent. Untreated cases can mature into month-scale avoidance.

**Differential diagnosis**
Rule out indifference. The key feature is not lack of care, but care becoming structurally unusable.

**Recommended treatment**
Send: “sorry, brain fog, replying now.” Observe the almost offensive normality of the outcome.

### 3. Phantom Notification Syndrome (PNS)

**Definition**  
A perceptual disturbance marked by the felt sensation of a phone buzz, chime, or ping despite the absence of any corresponding event.

**Diagnostic features**
- repeated checking of a silent device
- full confidence that something happened
- recurrence under anticipation, dread, or ambiguity

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
May intensify during romantic uncertainty, professional precarity, or periods of heightened social vigilance. Patients often report that the phone feels “possessed,” though the more likely explanation is simple nervous system mutiny.

**Recommended treatment**
Place phone out of reach for ten minutes and endure the strange empty weather of your own mind.

### 4. Search Query Spiral Disorder (SQSD)

**Definition**  
Escalation from one ordinary search into a chain of increasingly bizarre, adjacent, and epistemically unstable queries.

**Diagnostic features**
- the original question is forgotten within 15 minutes
- source quality deteriorates over time
- confidence decreases as search intensity increases
- person describes the process as “research” long after that becomes dishonest

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
The search spiral often begins in good faith and ends in a swamp of supplements, niche Reddit testimony, comparative mattress theology, or obscure forum posts from 2011.

**Recommended treatment**
Write down the original question. Answer only that question. Back away from the abyss in one smooth motion.

### 5. Autocorrect-Induced Rage Episodes (AIRE)

**Definition**  
Brief but spiritually intense anger triggered by a device repeatedly replacing correct words with nonsense, condescension, or social sabotage.

**Diagnostic features**
- repeated re-entry of the same correct word
- rising hostility toward inanimate software
- intrusive fantasy of throwing phone directly into sea

**Differential diagnosis**
Distinguish from generalized tech irritability by the presence of betrayal.

**Recommended treatment**
Retype slowly. Consider voice dictation. Reject violence, if possible.

### 6. Two-Factor Authentication Exhaustion (2FAE)

**Definition**  
Cumulative fatigue caused by repeated procedural demands to verify one’s identity to systems that remain fundamentally unconvinced.

**Diagnostic features**
- demoralization during ordinary login attempts
- repeated code retrieval across multiple devices
- sense of being accused by infrastructure

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
The patient often possesses the password, the phone, the code, and the sincerity, yet still experiences the interaction as existentially insulting.

**Recommended treatment**
Sit down before logging into anything consequential. Do not attempt taxes while standing.

## Section II. Identity, Aesthetics, and Vibes

### 7. Algorithmic Identity Diffusion (AID)

**Definition**  
Instability in taste and self-concept produced by prolonged exposure to recommendation systems and trend-conditioned self-recognition.

**Diagnostic features**
- uncertainty whether preferences are authentic or merely over-served
- rapid adoption of niche interests with suspicious platform timing
- diminished distinction between desire and exposure

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
Patients may describe an unsettling inability to determine whether they genuinely love linen, long-form interviews, sourdough, trail running, maximalist silver jewelry, or if these interests have merely been algorithmically composted into their self-image.

**Recommended treatment**
Like one thing privately. Tell no one. Protect it from branding.

### 8. Personal Brand Fragmentation (PBF)

**Definition**  
Distress caused by maintaining multiple partially incompatible selves across platforms, roles, and social ecologies.

**Diagnostic features**
- one self for work, another for friends, a third for romance, a fourth for professional networking, and no clear core model
- fatigue caused by cross-context tonal management
- fear that these selves may accidentally meet

**Course and prognosis**
Generally progressive during adulthood, especially under conditions of freelance labor, public posting, and networking.

**Recommended treatment**
Permit at least 15 percent consistency between contexts. A single recurring opinion can be stabilizing.

### 9. Ambiguous Slay Fatigue (ASF)

**Definition**  
A diffuse depletion state caused by ambient pressure to remain stylish, witty, emotionally evolved, physically maintained, culturally fluent, and somehow effortless.

**Diagnostic features**
- burnout without a single obvious cause
- resistance to further optimization discourse
- irritation at language such as “intentional,” “curated,” or “who is she”

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
The individual often reports not exactly wanting to die, but strongly wishing to stop being an ongoing project.

**Recommended treatment**
Wear the wrong socks. Eat a snack without documenting it. Embrace one ugly object.

### 10. Overcurated Self Presentation Syndrome (OSPS)

**Definition**  
A pattern of excessive self-editing that removes contradiction, spontaneity, and signs of ordinary creatureliness from outward expression.

**Diagnostic features**
- inability to post, send, or share anything not sanded to conceptual smoothness
- fear of being witnessed in draft form
- mistaken belief that coherence requires sterilization

**Recommended treatment**
Release one imperfect artifact into the world and do not immediately narrate its imperfections as self-defense.

### 11. Moodboard Displacement Complex (MDC)

**Definition**  
Replacement of actual making, deciding, or committing with endless collection of references, vibes, palettes, aspirations, and atmosphere fragments.

**Diagnostic features**
- abundance of inspiration with no conversion to action
- repeated phrase: “I’m still gathering references”
- emotional attachment to possibility over outcome

**Recommended treatment**
Choose one thing. Moodboards are courtship, not marriage.

### 12. Pathological Reinvention Urges (PRU)

**Definition**  
Recurring belief that the acquisition of a notebook, haircut, tote bag, planner, water bottle, app, routine, or desk lamp will cause a full personal rebirth.

**Diagnostic features**
- cyclical fresh-start fantasies
- shopping behaviors framed as existential restructuring
- spiritual overinvestment in containers and stationery

**Associated features supporting diagnosis**
Patients may show tenderness toward objects that symbolize future discipline. This tenderness is understandable and should be mocked gently.

**Recommended treatment**
Buy the notebook if needed, but admit clearly that the notebook is not a sacrament.

## Section III. Time Blindness and Calendar Crimes

### 13. Calendar Overconfidence Disorder (COD)

**Definition**  
A planning disorder marked by the sincere but unsupported belief that time is both abundant and obedient.

**Diagnostic features**
- scheduling multiple demanding tasks with no transition time
- assuming hunger, fatigue, transit, confusion, and grief will not occur
- repeated phrase: “it should be fine”

**Recommended treatment**
Cut the day’s ambitions by one third and call this realism, not surrender.

### 14. Sunday Evening Pre-Grief (SEPG)

**Definition**  
An anticipatory sorrow response beginning late Sunday and directed toward the already-felt emotional violence of Monday.

**Diagnostic features**
- atmospheric dread despite no immediate event
- pacing, laundry avoidance, low-level soul collapse
- resentment that time itself continues moving

**Recommended treatment**
Do one nice thing for Monday-self. This is not cure, but it is solidarity.

### 15. Errand Stacking Delusion (ESD)

**Definition**  
The false belief that multiple geographically irrational chores can together constitute “one quick trip.”

**Diagnostic features**
- route planning detached from real traffic, distance, or human stamina
- adding tasks mid-trip under the influence of optimism
- returning home offended by physics

**Recommended treatment**
Plan fewer stops. Respect mileage. Stop treating the city like a foldable map from 1987.

### 16. “Quick Little Task” Miscalculation Syndrome (QLTMS)

**Definition**  
A condition in which tasks described as small or quick reliably expand into bureaucratic, technical, or existential events.

**Diagnostic features**
- sentences beginning with “this will just take two seconds”
- immediate emergence of a missing login, strange form, or unclear instructions
- disproportionate emotional collapse by minute 17

**Recommended treatment**
Assume every task has a hidden second act.

### 17. Meeting Recovery Lag (MRL)

**Definition**  
A post-meeting cognitive depletion state in which the body remains upright but the mind has filed an internal complaint.

**Diagnostic features**
- inability to do focused work immediately after calls
- staring at screen while soul rehydrates
- brief hostility toward calendar itself

**Recommended treatment**
Schedule buffer time, or at minimum stop pretending the next meeting begins with a full nervous system.

### 18. Inbox Resurrection Anxiety (IRA)

**Definition**  
Distress triggered by reopening an email thread that should by all ethical and narrative logic have died naturally.

**Diagnostic features**
- dread before opening old thread
- scanning for whether blame has matured while unread
- temptation to disappear into agriculture instead

**Recommended treatment**
Open it. Answer plainly. Resist writing 4 paragraphs to explain your humanity.

## Section IV. Device-Bonded Dysregulations

### 19. Low-Battery Existentiality (LBE)

**Definition**  
An acute mood syndrome triggered by device battery decline below a psychologically destabilizing threshold.

**Diagnostic features**
- noticeable emotional deterioration below 20 percent
- irrational urgency below 10 percent
- narrowed capacity to imagine a future

**Recommended treatment**
Charge the device before diagnosing your life.

### 20. Charger Dependency Panic (CDP)

**Definition**  
Disproportionate alarm upon realizing that one’s immediate functionality depends on locating a single specific cable.

**Diagnostic features**
- searching rooms with disaster intensity
- contempt for incompatible connectors
- treating cable possession as civilization itself

**Recommended treatment**
Own more than one charger and stop living like a fugitive from electricity.

### 21. Screen Rotation Betrayal Disorder (SRBD)

**Definition**  
A trust rupture produced by unwanted device rotation at precisely the wrong moment.

**Diagnostic features**
- involuntary exclamation upon rotation
- body repositioning escalating problem rather than solving it
- deep feeling that device has chosen sides against you

**Recommended treatment**
Turn on orientation lock and reclaim legal authority.

### 22. Bluetooth Pairing Fury (BPF)

**Definition**  
Intense resentment produced when two devices designed to connect refuse to acknowledge each other’s existence.

**Diagnostic features**
- repeated toggling of Bluetooth with priestly desperation
- speaking to device as though shame might work
- sudden fantasies of analog life

**Recommended treatment**
Restart everything. If that fails, pronounce a curse and use a cable.

### 23. Password Reset Despair (PRD)

**Definition**  
Demoralization associated with producing yet another password variation from the same stale symbolic ruins.

**Diagnostic features**
- rejected password attempts containing increasingly humiliated punctuation
- loss of self-respect during security-question phase
- suspicious nostalgia for simpler forms of danger

**Recommended treatment**
Use a password manager and cease hand-crafting these little cryptographic tragedies.

### 24. Software Update Suspicion Syndrome (SUSS)

**Definition**  
A defensive stance toward updates based on the historically justified belief that the phrase “bug fixes and improvements” may conceal betrayal.

**Diagnostic features**
- postponing updates until social proof emerges
- dread that familiar functions will be moved, removed, or spiritually degraded
- belief that every improvement costs three hidden annoyances

**Recommended treatment**
Update one non-essential device first and let it serve as a sacrificial scout.

## Section V. Social and Interpersonal Disturbances

### 25. Voice Note Avoidance Syndrome (VNAS)

**Definition**  
Avoidance of incoming audio messages due to their demand for uninterrupted time, auditory submission, and a level of situational readiness not always available.

**Diagnostic features**
- delaying playback indefinitely
- wishing sender had simply used words with edges
- resentment increasing with message length

**Recommended treatment**
If the note exceeds 90 seconds, you may request a summary without shame.

### 26. Group Chat Peripheral Guilt (GCPG)

**Definition**  
A low-grade shame condition produced by seeing everything in a group chat, contributing nothing, and remaining socially spectral.

**Diagnostic features**
- chronic lurking with affectionate intent
- fear that reentry now requires an excellent joke
- periodic reaction-only compensation

**Recommended treatment**
Send one simple message. You are not reentering parliament.

### 27. Seen-Zone Hyperinterpretation (SZH)

**Definition**  
Compulsive analysis of read receipts, response timing, punctuation, reaction choices, and silence as evidence of relational destabilization.

**Diagnostic features**
- assigning meaning to ellipses, lowercase, and timestamp gaps
- inability to tolerate ambiguous delay
- repeated consultation of friends as informal forensic linguists

**Recommended treatment**
Assume the other person has a sink, a body, and an email account. Most pauses are infrastructural, not tragic.

### 28. Courtesy Laugh Burnout (CLB)

**Definition**  
Emotional fatigue caused by repeatedly signaling amusement for the maintenance of social smoothness after the spirit of laughter has left the body.

**Diagnostic features**
- reflexive “lol” divorced from joy
- cheeks participating while soul abstains
- depletion following extended workplace banter

**Recommended treatment**
Permit one neutral response where previously you would have performed delight.

### 29. Mutual Scheduling Collapse (MSC)

**Definition**  
Breakdown of social maintenance caused by the impossibility of aligning adult calendars with sufficient energy on both sides.

**Diagnostic features**
- affection remains high while meetings fail repeatedly
- month-ahead planning for coffee
- eventual collapse into voice memo promises and vague hope

**Recommended treatment**
Schedule badly but concretely. “Wednesday 7?” has saved more friendships than shared yearning.

### 30. Excessive “No Worries!” Compensation (ENWC)

**Definition**  
Pathological over-softening of language in order to conceal legitimate inconvenience, disappointment, irritation, or contained fury.

**Diagnostic features**
- saying “no worries” when worries are in fact plentiful
- sentence-level emotional laundering
- fear that directness constitutes violence

**Recommended treatment**
Try: “all good, but next time please let me know earlier.” This is survivable.

## Section VI. Cognitive Wear and Tear

### 31. Background Doomscroll Dissociation (BDD)

**Definition**  
A trance-like absorption in distressing content that leaves the individual less informed, less calm, and more psychically frayed.

**Diagnostic features**
- intended check becomes long unchosen session
- fragmented memory of consumed material
- increased dread with little corresponding knowledge

**Recommended treatment**
Close the app before you achieve a perfect awareness of horrors you cannot act on tonight.

### 32. Open-Plan Attention Shredding (OPAS)

**Definition**  
Cognitive fragmentation caused by attempting focused thought in an environment optimized for interruption, visibility, and acoustic irritation.

**Diagnostic features**
- inability to sustain conceptual work in shared office conditions
- involuntary awareness of all nearby snack wrappers and conversations
- mounting fantasy of solitary hut

**Recommended treatment**
Use headphones, barriers, or strategic disappearance. Not all openness is humane.

### 33. Thumbnail-Induced Memory Confusion (TIMC)

**Definition**  
False confidence that one has understood a piece of content because one recognizes its visual packaging.

**Diagnostic features**
- saying “I know that” based solely on thumbnail familiarity
- inability to reproduce any actual substance
- conflation of recognition with comprehension

**Recommended treatment**
Ask yourself to summarize the thing. If you cannot, do not claim psychic ownership.

### 34. Context-Switch Concussion, Mild (CSC-M)

**Definition**  
Temporary intellectual stun caused by repeated rapid movement between tasks, topics, interfaces, and registers.

**Diagnostic features**
- sensation of brain buffers clearing mid-afternoon
- loss of continuity across work blocks
- inability to reconstitute intent after interruption

**Recommended treatment**
Cluster similar tasks. Guard transitions. Stop pretending the mind is a browser tab with infinite instant restore.

### 35. Notification-Triggered Thought Evaporation (NTTE)

**Definition**  
Immediate disappearance of a meaningful thought following interruption by a notification, request, ping, or side demand.

**Diagnostic features**
- active thought vanishes upon alert
- person attempts reconstruction with visible grief
- replacement thought is usually administrative and worse

**Recommended treatment**
Keep a capture note open. Your thoughts deserve witness protection.

### 36. Tab Return Amnesia (TRA)

**Definition**  
Failure to recall the original purpose of an already-open browser tab upon returning to it later.

**Diagnostic features**
- reopening tab with hope followed by emptiness
- no memory of why article, product, map, or document mattered
- defensive refusal to close tab “just in case it comes back”

**Recommended treatment**
If the intention cannot be recalled, the tab is now a relic. Release it with honors.

## Section VII. Archival and Possession Disorders

### 37. Screenshot Hoarding Disorder (SHD)

**Definition**  
Compulsive saving of digital fragments for later use in systems that do not materially exist.

**Diagnostic features**
- screenshots of recipes, jokes, outfits, maps, tweets, apartment listings, and emotional evidence
- no tagging system and no retrieval discipline
- vague belief that deletion constitutes personal diminishment

**Recommended treatment**
Delete 30 screenshots in a row. If you survive, continue.

### 38. Note-App Fossilization (NAF)

**Definition**  
Accumulation of abandoned lists, fragments, half-plans, affirmations, revelations, and logistics into a stratified textual bog.

**Diagnostic features**
- hundreds of notes, few revisited intentionally
- titles such as “ideas,” “new plan,” “actually important,” or “for real this time”
- inability to locate anything without scrolling through former selves

**Recommended treatment**
Merge, archive, or delete with mercy. Not every fragment is sacred.

### 39. Downloads Folder Denial (DFD)

**Definition**  
An avoidance pattern characterized by refusal to inspect the directory most likely to reveal the truth of one’s digital behavior.

**Diagnostic features**
- desktop cleanliness coexisting with download chaos
- anxiety about filenames generated by machines
- belief that not looking preserves innocence

**Recommended treatment**
Open the folder in daylight. Sort by date. Accept history.

### 40. “I Might Need This PDF” Syndrome (IMNTPS)

**Definition**  
Inability to delete unread files because they symbolize a hypothetical future self who is diligent, scholarly, and likely imaginary.

**Diagnostic features**
- excessive retention of papers, manuals, reports, ebooks, and receipts
- emotional attachment to latent potential
- confusion between ownership and digestion

**Recommended treatment**
Keep only what has a plausible future use. The rest are not opportunities, they are paper ghosts.

### 41. Sentimental Receipt Retention (SRR)

**Definition**  
Preservation of trivial transactional artifacts due to vague emotion, weak trust in institutions, or diffuse tax paranoia.

**Diagnostic features**
- old receipts in bags, drawers, coat pockets, and books
- inability to explain why they remain
- discomfort when asked to discard them

**Recommended treatment**
If it does not serve taxes, memory, or warranty, let the tiny paper die.

### 42. Link Saving Without Reading Disorder (LSWRD)

**Definition**  
Compulsive bookmarking of articles, essays, papers, and resources as a substitute for actual engagement.

**Diagnostic features**
- impressive read-later collections with little reading
- pleasure spike on saving link mistaken for learning
- belief that collecting pathways equals walking them

**Recommended treatment**
Read one saved link before saving three more. This ratio is medicinal.

## Section VIII. Emerging Conditions Requiring Further Study

### 43. AI Prompt Overfitting Personality Drift (AIPOPD)

**Definition**  
Gradual adoption of strange rhetorical habits, synthetic politeness structures, and oddly optimized phrasing following prolonged interaction with language models.

**Diagnostic features**
- speaking in prompts
- asking humans for output in bullet points and sections
- weirdly formal gratitude toward appliances and bots

**Recommended treatment**
Talk to one messy human and tolerate their noncompliance.

### 44. Food Delivery Menu Exhaustion Collapse (FDMEC)

**Definition**  
Decision paralysis produced by staring at too many mediocre food options while already too hungry to think clearly.

**Diagnostic features**
- escalating standards as glucose falls
- inability to choose despite abundant choices
- eventual ordering of the same thing with resentment

**Recommended treatment**
Maintain a stable top three. Hunger is not the hour for imaginative democracy.

### 45. Excessive Micro-Optimization Disorder (EMOD)

**Definition**  
Compulsive refinement of trivial systems in order to avoid confronting larger, scarier, and more meaningful problems.

**Diagnostic features**
- spending 90 minutes perfecting task software instead of doing task
- fascination with workflows under conditions of unfinished actual work
- spiritual attachment to system tuning

**Recommended treatment**
Before improving the workflow, do 20 minutes of the work the workflow claims to support.

### 46. Playlist Identity Rigidity (PIR)

**Definition**  
The belief that one’s playlists must perfectly encode a stable and impressive self despite overwhelming evidence that the self is mixed, repetitive, and mood-dependent.

**Diagnostic features**
- overediting playlists for narrative coherence
- shame about chaotic listening history
- assigning moral value to sonic transitions

**Recommended treatment**
Make one playlist called “songs I currently tolerate.” Heal through accuracy.

### 47. Wellness Information Saturation Fatigue (WISF)

**Definition**  
Exhaustion caused by exposure to too many contradictory instructions for how to sleep, eat, move, heal, focus, regulate hormones, calm inflammation, and become an optimal mammal.

**Diagnostic features**
- inability to distinguish evidence from vibes
- guilt generated by mutually incompatible advice
- suspicion that every simple act now requires a protocol

**Recommended treatment**
Pick a few basics and stop letting strangers narrate your breakfast as a moral referendum.

### 48. Chronic Ambient Administrative Dread (CAAD)

**Definition**  
A persistent low hum of anxiety generated by forms, portals, renewals, subscriptions, billing cycles, account notices, verification loops, and unnamed obligations waiting just offscreen.

**Diagnostic features**
- waking with sense of forgotten duty
- unease triggered by envelopes, subject lines, and official fonts
- inability to fully relax because some task is probably lurking

**Course and prognosis**
Worsens during tax season, moving, healthcare navigation, job transitions, and any encounter with systems requiring uploaded PDFs.

**Recommended treatment**
Make a list. Convert fog into items. Administration feeds on vagueness.

## General Severity Specifiers

Use the following specifiers when describing symptom intensity across disorders:

- *Mild:* the person remains functional and can joke about the condition in real time.  
- *Moderate:* the condition visibly shapes behavior, scheduling, mood, or relationships.  
- *Severe:* daily life must route around the symptom cluster; the person has begun building rituals or infrastructure to survive it.  
- *With insight:* the patient knows exactly what is happening and continues anyway.  
- *Without insight:* the patient calls the condition “just how things are now.”

## General Treatment Principles

1. Hydrate before theorizing.  
2. Eat before escalating.  
3. Reduce exposure to app-designed psychic warfare.  
4. Prefer direct language over tonal guesswork when stakes are modest.  
5. Distinguish tools from identities.  
6. Treat friction as information, not proof of personal failure.  
7. Accept “good enough” whenever perfection is functioning as delay.  
8. Go outside if the walls start feeling editorial.  
9. Ask whether the problem is emotional, logistical, nutritional, or merely one password too many.  
10. When in doubt, do the next small real thing instead of constructing a philosophy of why you cannot.

## Appendix A. Clinical Intake Questions

When evaluating a patient for extremely contemporary disorders, the clinician may ask:

- How many tabs are open right now, and be honest.  
- Do you currently owe anyone a text message that has become symbolically overcharged?  
- At what battery percentage does your personality change?  
- How many unread saved articles do you believe are part of your intelligence?  
- Have you recently mistaken researching for living?  
- Do you possess a folder, drawer, bag, or app that functions mainly as an anxiety sediment trap?  
- When someone sends a voice note over 2 minutes, what happens inside your body?  
- How many “quick” tasks have betrayed you this week?  
- Do you believe a new notebook could save you?  
- Is your problem today existential, administrative, or glucose-related?

## Appendix B. Differential Diagnosis Cheat Sheet

*CTPD vs TRA*  
CTPD concerns the accumulation of tabs. TRA concerns forgetting why a given tab exists.

*RDP vs GCPG*  
RDP occurs in one-to-one or direct communications where reply pressure becomes disabling. GCPG concerns silence within many-to-many social spaces.

*ASF vs WISF*  
ASF centers aesthetic and performative exhaustion. WISF centers optimization and health-information overload.

*EMOD vs MDC*  
EMOD perfects systems instead of doing substantive work. MDC gathers references instead of choosing or making.

*CAAD vs IRA*  
CAAD is broad, ambient bureaucratic dread. IRA is the sharp pain of one undead thread returning to demand tribute.

## Appendix C. Sample Case Vignettes

*Case 1: The Tabs*  
A 31-year-old knowledge worker presents with 86 open browser tabs, six active note-taking systems, and a belief that closing anything would constitute “losing momentum.” Prognosis is guarded but not hopeless.

*Case 2: The Text*  
A patient reports caring deeply about a friend, mentally composing a warm reply on Tuesday, and sending nothing until the following Monday after consulting two confidants and three emotional weather systems.

*Case 3: The Menu*  
A hungry adult spends 24 minutes evaluating food delivery options, rejects 17 restaurants for reasons that become increasingly spiritual, and ultimately orders the same noodles with resentment.

*Case 4: The Monday*  
On Sunday at 4:40 PM, an otherwise healthy individual develops a sense that life is already closing in despite still wearing weekend clothes and having touched no actual Monday obligations.

## Closing Note

The authors of this handbook emphasize that many conditions described herein are not private defects so much as intelligible responses to environments built with suspicious hostility toward attention, embodiment, rest, and ordinary human limits.

The reader is therefore encouraged to emerge from this volume with slightly more mercy for the self, slightly less reverence for interfaces, and a newly sharpened suspicion that many problems currently felt as individual failure are, in fact, design crimes.

If symptoms persist, try water, food, a walk, one honest text, fewer tabs, and a brief refusal to optimize.

That is all the committee can responsibly recommend.
